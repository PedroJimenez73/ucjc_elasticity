3 Demand elasticity and revenue

Any shock that changes the equilibrium price to change will affect the industryâ€™s revenue. And accordingly, the elasticity of demand will determine the effect that a price rise (or fall) can have on the total income.

Whether the revenue rises or falls when the equilibrium price increases depends on the elasticity of demand.

  • With elastic demand, a higher price reduces revenue.
  • With inelastic demand, a higher price increases revenue.

A rise in prices causes a greater percentage drop in the quantity consumed, so that the final total income (A + C) is less than before the change in price (A + B). Capital loss.

However, in the case of perfectly inelastic demands, the rise in the price does not cause falls in the quantity demanded and the final income (A + C) is greater than the initial (A). Capital increase.
